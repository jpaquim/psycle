Modifying your sound

Now, your sound might be a bit plain and boring at the moment, but there's plenty of work to be done yet. Firstly, we can change the parameters of the synthesiser. To do this, double click on a non-active part of your generator or press Shift+Enter while it is selected. This brings up a window where you can edit the individual parameters of the machine.

There are several ways to change them. First, you can click on a knob and drag it to the top or the bottom of the screen.(Do not try to rotate it, it is just up or down)
If the parameter has many values, it may be difficult to select one, so, while dragging, you can press the Control key to make it go slower or even the Shift key, to make it move one position at a time.
There's a second way to change the value, which is right click on one of the knobs. This will popup a window where you can write the exact value.

Adding Effects

There's one other major type of modification we can make to our sound besides changing the parameters of the generator - we can add effects. Press F9 again to bring up the "Add Machine" window, and expand the "Effects" list. Go down and find "Arguru Reverb", select it and click OK.
To use this effect on our generator, we need to make the sound go through it: Right click the white triangle connecting the generator to the Master Machine, and drag it over to the effect you've just added. You will now see that the wire has moved!
Now, simply right click and drag from the effect to the Master Machine to add a new wire, and try playing some notes again. Notice the difference?

We can edit the parameters of the effect in the same way as we did for the generator, by double clicking the machine.
