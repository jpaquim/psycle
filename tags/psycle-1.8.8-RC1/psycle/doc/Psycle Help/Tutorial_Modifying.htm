Modifying your sound

Now, your sound might be a bit plain and boring at the moment, but there's plenty of work to be done yet. Firstly, we can change the parameters of the synthesiser. To do this, double click on a non-active part of your generator. This brings up a window where you can edit the individual parameters of the machine.

If you don't know much about sound synthesis and what the parameters do, read the "Synth Secrets" series (google it, you'll find it). Here there are some parameters for Arguru Synth to start you off (Most of the times, you'll just change a reduced group of them):

Adding Effects

There's one other major type of modification we can make to our sound besides changing the parameters of the generator - we can add effects. Press F9 again to bring up the "Add Machine" window, and expand the "Effects" list. Go down and find "Arguru Reverb", select it and click OK.
To use this effect on our generator, we need to make the sound go through it: Right click the white triangle connecting the generator to the Master Machine, and drag it over to the effect you've just added. You will now see that the wire has moved!
Now, simply right click and drag from the effect to the Master Machine to add a new wire, and try playing some notes again. Notice the difference?

We can edit the parameters of the effect in the same way as we did for the generator, by double clicking the machine.
